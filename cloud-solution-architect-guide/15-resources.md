## Books (Priority Order)

1. **Designing Data-Intensive Applications** — Martin Kleppmann
   - Why: Best book on distributed systems, databases, consistency
   - Read: Before any system design interview

2. **Kubernetes in Action** — Marko Luksa
   - Why: Deep Kubernetes understanding
   - Read: After basic Kubernetes knowledge

3. **Building Microservices** — Sam Newman
   - Why: Microservices patterns, DDD, service boundaries
   - Read: For microservices architecture interviews

4. **The Phoenix Project** — Gene Kim
   - Why: DevOps culture, understanding business impact
   - Read: For leadership and culture conversations

5. **Software Architecture: The Hard Parts** — Neal Ford et al.
   - Why: Modern distributed architecture trade-offs
   - Read: For principal architect level

6. **Fundamentals of Software Architecture** — Mark Richards & Neal Ford
   - Why: Architecture styles, decision-making
   - Read: For architect mindset

7. **Site Reliability Engineering** — Google (free: sre.google/books)
   - Why: SLOs, error budgets, reliability engineering
   - Read: For observability and reliability conversations

8. **Cloud Native Patterns** — Cornelia Davis
   - Why: Cloud-native design patterns
   - Read: For cloud-native architecture interviews
